Theorem 1: Prime Channel Avoidance

For prime modulus p, all residues 1, 2, ..., p-1 are coprime to p.

No fraction r/p reduces to simpler form 1/N (N > 1).

Geometric Interpretation: Prime moduli form isolated coprime manifolds in the modular lattice, avoiding all Farey flow lines.

Theorem 2: Composite Channel Projection

For composite M, each residue r reduces to r'/M' where d = gcd(r,M), M' = M/d, r' = r/d.

Reducible residues populate Farey channels (1/N and rational multiples).

Channel Denominators: Exactly the proper divisors of M.
